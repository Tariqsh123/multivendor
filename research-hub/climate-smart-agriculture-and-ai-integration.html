Climate-Smart Agriculture & AI Integration: A Strategic Pathway for Pakistan’s Food Security

By Prof. Nadeem Ahmed Faraz

Agriculture contributes nearly 23% to Pakistan’s GDP and employs over 37% of the national workforce, making it the backbone of the economy. Yet, Pakistan ranks among the top ten countries most vulnerable to climate change. Rising temperatures, unpredictable monsoons, glacial melt, and extreme floods — particularly the devastating 2022 floods that affected over 33 million people and damaged more than 4 million acres of crops — underscore the urgency for transformation.

Climate-Smart Agriculture (CSA), as defined by the FAO, aims to increase productivity, enhance resilience, and reduce greenhouse gas emissions simultaneously. Studies indicate CSA practices such as precision irrigation, climate-resilient seed varieties, and soil management can improve yields by 15–30% while reducing water use by up to 20–40%.

The integration of Artificial Intelligence (AI) accelerates this transformation. AI-driven weather forecasting, satellite imaging, predictive analytics, and smart sensors enable farmers to optimize irrigation, detect crop diseases early, and forecast yields with higher accuracy. The World Bank projects that digital agriculture solutions could increase farm productivity in developing countries by up to 25% by 2030.

For Pakistan, combining CSA with AI is not merely technological advancement — it is a strategic necessity to ensure food security, climate resilience, rural prosperity, and sustainable economic growth toward 2050.
