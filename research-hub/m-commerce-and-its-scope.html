M-Commerce & Its Scope: Growth, Trends, and 2030 Projections

By Prof. Nadeem Ahmed Faraz

Mobile Commerce (M-Commerce) has emerged as the dominant force in digital retail, reshaping consumer behavior, business strategies, and global economic projections. With smartphone penetration exceeding 5.6 billion global subscriptions in 2023 and estimated to reach 6.3 billion by 2030, mobile devices are set to drive the majority of online commerce in the next decade.

The global M-Commerce market, valued at approximately US$678.2 billion in 2024, is forecast to expand to US$2.4 trillion by 2030, representing a CAGR of ~23.7%—one of the fastest growth rates across digital industries. This explosive growth is fueled by mobile-first consumer behavior, app-centric shopping experiences, and seamless payment systems. In 2025, mobile devices were projected to account for more than 73% of total e-commerce sales, highlighting the clear shift away from traditional desktop browsing.

Regional dynamics demonstrate Asia-Pacific as a mobile commerce powerhouse, while North America continues to see robust growth through personalized apps and integrated digital wallets. Enhanced connectivity, particularly with 5G adoption, will further accelerate transaction speeds, security, and user engagement.

By 2030, M-Commerce will not only dominate retail transactions but also extend into services, digital content, and financial payments, creating vast opportunities for businesses willing to innovate. School leaders, educators, and future workforce planners must recognize this shift: digital literacy and mobile commerce understanding will be core competencies for tomorrow's economy. The mobile economy is not just growing—it is defining the digital marketplace by 2030.
